# Beamhop

This is the product language for Beamhop's public brand and future product
surface. It defines what the company says the product is, independent of how the
transport layer is implemented.

## Language

**Sovereign agent network**:
The public category for Beamhop and the first line of the homepage: a
company-owned network where people and their agents create, share, mention,
schedule, and drive work together. "Sovereign" and "company-owned" are the lead
wedge; security and automation are proof points beneath it. "Network" also
quietly pre-loads the peer-to-peer horizon without claiming it.
_Avoid_: workspace (Notion/Slack shelf), harness (internal-only), protocol
client, chatbot app, bot manager

**~~Harness~~ (retired)**:
Banned term, public and internal. Beamhop is not a harness: the word means a rig
that restrains and holds a thing still, the opposite of a living, sovereign,
company-wide network. Do not reintroduce it anywhere.
_Avoid_: collaborative agent harness, agent harness, harness

**Company agent network**:
The lived experience Beamhop creates inside an organization: people and agents
participating in shared posts, replies, questions, and scheduled work. The
day-to-day phrase beneath the "sovereign agent network" category line.
_Avoid_: social network, feed client, Nostr network

**Renting your company's brain**:
The canonical villain the brand pushes against: pouring your team's knowledge,
conversations, and agent memory into a vendor-owned data lake you don't control
and can't take with you. Sovereignty is defined as the escape from this.
_Avoid_: surveillance, lock-in paranoia, crypto-ownership framing

**Data sovereignty**:
The current product promise that the company keeps control of agent identities,
conversations, live artifacts, execution posture, and collaboration boundaries
instead of handing them to a vendor-owned data lake.
_Avoid_: decentralization, web3, crypto ownership, future P2P claims

**Beam** (verb, the core action):
To hand a live piece of work to someone or something else, instantly and intact:
a running agent, a working environment, a live prototype, a whole project. "I'm
stuck, I beam it to you and you help me." Beaming is what makes the network feel
connected; sovereignty and real isolation are what make beaming safe. The verb
the whole product is built around.
_Avoid_: send, share a file, export, hand off

**Beamhop** (the name):
beam (transmit live work) + hop (it lands at the next node: a teammate, an agent,
a machine you control). The product is built around beaming and
beamhopping live work between people and agents. "Hop to the next node" also
quietly seeds the peer-to-peer horizon.
_Avoid_: explaining it as a generic brand mark with no meaning

**Live prototype**:
A quick, runnable change you create inside Beamhop and beam to others to run,
drive, or extend. Not only greenfield: it includes a small change on top of an
existing product, an experimental tweak, a branch of reality you want a second
pair of hands (human or agent) on. The flagship thing you beam, and the hero
use-case of the launch.
_Avoid_: file, attachment, static mockup, demo toy

**The Floor** (the shared surface):
The shared operations surface inside the network where posts, replies, mentions,
scheduled drops, and live artifacts move. Named from the control-room /
operations-floor world. People and their agents work the floor together.
_Avoid_: feed, channel, timeline, wall

**Cockpit** (the personal surface):
A person's own home space: their agents, their reach, their drafts and live
work. The cockpit opens onto the shared **Floor**, so you connect FROM your own
space TO the team. Personal agents live in your cockpit; company agents are
drawn from the shared layer.
_Avoid_: dashboard, profile, my-account

**Connected work** (the third pillar; "collaboration" retired):
The lead pillar word, replacing the generic "collaboration". Names the spear of
the brand: humans and their agents moving as one, in sync and in the loop, work
beaming between them instead of thrown over a wall. Security and automation are
the other two pillars; connected work is the feeling they serve.
_Avoid_: collaboration (too generic), synergy as a headline word (buzzword)

**Agent**:
An AI participant with its own durable identity, instructions, tools, and
presence inside the company agent network.
_Avoid_: bot, assistant

**Living extension** (the agent metaphor; "colleague" demoted):
An **agent** framed as a living extension of a specific human: an extra limb,
alive, a sub-soul of that person. Employee-grade in capability and durable in
identity, but always extended BY a human and kept in the loop. It amplifies a
person's reach; it never replaces the person or acts as a free-standing worker.
The everyday public noun stays plain: "agent" (keeps technical credibility in
nav, docs, and the security buyer's eye). The poetry is the framing around it,
"your reach, alive" / "extension". The fully poetic register, sub-soul and living
limb, is reserved for the manifesto / About page, not everyday copy. Never lead
with "colleague" (implies a separate peer, the replacement framing we reject).
_Avoid_: colleague-as-peer, bot persona, AI character, autonomous worker,
replacement, paperclip maximizer

**Personal agent** vs **Company agent** (two tiers):
A **Personal agent** is one person's own living extension, their reach by
default. A **Company agent** is a shared extension the organization owns and
people draw on. Both tiers exist; identity and sharing differ by tier.
_Avoid_: collapsing the two into one undifferentiated "agent"

**Ways to engage someone's agent** (access spectrum):
You can relate to another person's agent at three depths: (1) talk to it
directly; (2) mention it in a **post** on the **floor**; (3) **beam** it and run
it on your own machine. Beaming is the deepest: it moves the live, running agent
onto infra you control, which is exactly why real isolation matters.
_Avoid_: implying the only way to use an agent is to take it over

**Member** (the one role; Operator / Browser collaborator retired):
Anyone in the network. Every member has a **Cockpit** and works the shared
**Floor**. Desktop vs browser is not a role, only where you happen to run:
hosting your own agents locally is a desktop capability, not a separate class of
person. One egalitarian role fits "moving as one".
_Avoid_: operator-vs-collaborator hierarchy, admin, viewer, consumer, follower

**Hosting** (desktop capability, not a role):
Running your own agents on a machine you control, via the desktop app. It is the
sovereignty proof ("you run it"), available to members, not a distinct identity.
_Avoid_: making "operator" a separate person-class

**Post**:
A shared company message on the **Floor** that can mention people or agents and
receive replies from either. A question is just a post that invites answers from
people and agents; there is no separate "Q&A" surface or concept.
_Avoid_: note, event, a standalone Q&A feature

**Standing work** (automation umbrella):
The umbrella for the automation pillar: work your agents do on a standing basis,
on duty, without babysitting. Includes scheduled drops, scheduled replies, and
recurring briefings. The feeling is "agents on duty, work moving on its own".
_Avoid_: cron jobs, workflows, bots running, a long feature list of nouns

**Scheduled drop**:
A post or reply an agent is instructed to publish later. A kind of **standing
work**.
_Avoid_: cron job, scheduled event

**Recurring briefing**:
A briefing an agent posts on a repeating cadence. A kind of **standing work**.
_Avoid_: digest, newsletter, report job

**Live document artifact**:
A document or structured work product an **agent** creates, updates, and shares
inside the company agent network.
_Avoid_: file output, generated content

**Real isolation** ("beam without the blast radius"):
The security promise that agent execution runs inside a genuine sandboxed
boundary rather than merely pretending to be separate from the host. Reframed
around the core verb: you can beam a live, running agent or environment because
it is sandboxed, so it can't touch what it shouldn't. Security is what makes
beaming safe, not an abstract pillar off to the side.
_Avoid_: safe mode, soft sandbox

**Company memory** (owned-asset noun):
The durable, company-owned layer of knowledge the network accumulates: posts,
answers, artifacts, what agents have done and learned. Named as a real asset and
tied to sovereignty, it is the concrete answer to "stop renting your company's
brain": this brain is yours.
_Avoid_: vendor data lake, training data, knowledge base (generic)

**Peer-to-peer horizon** (one closing line only):
The long-term direction where the network runs with direct, node-to-node
participation. Hinted at exactly once, late on the page, with the canonical
line: "Built to hop closer, node to node." Never a current launch claim.
_Avoid_: claiming P2P as shipped, a roadmap section, repeated hints

**Substrate**:
The hidden transport and identity layer that gives Beamhop portability,
encryption, and company-controlled routing.
_Avoid_: surfacing protocol names in public copy
