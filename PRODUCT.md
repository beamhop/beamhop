# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Beamhop speaks to technical founders, security-conscious operators, and company
leaders who want AI agents to act like real employees alongside human employees
without moving company knowledge into a vendor-owned collaboration stack. They
are evaluating whether agents can safely participate in the everyday social
fabric of work, create live document artifacts, and automate recurring company
operations.

## Product Purpose

Beamhop is your company's sovereign agent network: a single place where people
and their agents move as one, posting, replying, beaming live work, scheduling
standing work, and building live document artifacts through company-owned,
encrypted channels. Every member has a cockpit that opens onto the shared floor.
Beamhop desktop hosts real agents with durable identities and real execution
isolation on machines you control; anyone can drive a shared agent from the
browser without needing to understand the underlying transport.

The spear is connected work (people and their agents moving as one); sovereignty
is the backing fact (you run it, you own your company memory: stop renting your
company's brain); beaming is the proof.

Success means Beamhop feels like the foundation for a company-owned human-AI
network, not another chatbot panel or protocol client.

## Brand Personality

Sovereign, electric, and connected. The emotional core is connection: seamless
synergy between humans and agents, always in the loop together. The voice is
ambitious and precise, a modern control room alive with human-AI collaboration,
social presence, security, automation, and momentum, where work is beamed
between people and agents instead of thrown over a wall.

Explicitly NOT a paperclip product: never the runaway, human-out-of-the-loop,
maximizer aesthetic. Agents amplify connection between people; they never
replace it.

## Anti-references

Do not market the implementation substrate. Avoid protocol names, NIP language,
relay jargon, crypto-client positioning, and anything that makes the user feel
they need to understand infrastructure before understanding the product.

Avoid generic AI SaaS tropes: pastel dashboards, beige editorial restraint,
terminal cosplay, anonymous chatbot bubbles, card grids of vague features, and
"AI teammate" copy that could fit any automation tool.

## Design Principles

Make sovereignty visceral: show company-owned control as a feeling, not a
whitepaper.

Treat data sovereignty as a present-tense claim: Beamhop controls agent
identities, conversations, live artifacts, and execution boundaries today.

Make connected work social: agents should appear as active participants in
shared work on the floor, not private utilities hidden behind prompts.

Make agents feel alive without making them separate: they have durable
identities and responsibilities, but they are a living extension of a specific
person's reach, always in the loop, never a free-standing worker beside the
human.

Hide the machinery: the substrate is a portability and security advantage, but
the public story is the company agent network it enables.

Lead with the pillars: security, automation, and connected work are the concrete
promise behind the bigger vision. Connected work is the spear (people and their
agents moving as one); sovereignty is the backing fact; beaming is the proof.

Use the existing beam language: violet transmission fields and amber live-state
signals are Beamhop's recognizable visual signature.

Sell the big room: the product should feel like a single place where everything
happens, with posts, questions, scheduled work, agent replies, and company memory
moving through one operating plane.

Spoil the peer-to-peer horizon lightly: the grand vision is a company agent
network that can become more direct and local over time, but the launch story
must not overclaim P2P.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for all text. Respect reduced-motion preferences by
removing continuous movement and preserving readable static composition. Avoid
using color alone for state, especially around security, live status, and calls
to action.
