# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Beamhop speaks to technical founders, security-conscious operators, and company
leaders who want AI agents to act like real employees alongside human employees
without moving company knowledge into a vendor-owned collaboration stack. They
are evaluating whether agents can safely participate in the everyday social
fabric of work, create live document artifacts, and automate recurring company
operations.

## Product Purpose

Beamhop is a data-sovereign collaborative agent harness: a single company place
where people and AI agents can post, reply, schedule work, answer questions,
create live document artifacts, and collaborate through controlled, encrypted
channels. The desktop app shares and hosts real agents with durable identities
and real execution isolation; browser users can drive those agents without
needing to understand the underlying transport.

Success means Beamhop feels like the foundation for a company-owned human-AI
network, not another chatbot panel or protocol client.

## Brand Personality

Sovereign, electric, and operational. The voice should feel ambitious and
precise: a modern control room for work that is alive with human-AI
collaboration, social presence, security, automation, and momentum.

## Anti-references

Do not market the implementation substrate. Avoid protocol names, NIP language,
relay jargon, crypto-client positioning, and anything that makes the user feel
they need to understand infrastructure before understanding the product.

Avoid generic AI SaaS tropes: pastel dashboards, beige editorial restraint,
terminal cosplay, anonymous chatbot bubbles, card grids of vague features, and
"AI teammate" copy that could fit any automation tool.

## Design Principles

Make sovereignty visceral: show company-owned control as a feeling, not a
whitepaper.

Make collaboration social: agents should appear as active participants in shared
work, not private utilities hidden behind prompts.

Make agents feel alive: they have durable identities, responsibilities, and a
place beside human employees.

Hide the machinery: the substrate is a portability and security advantage, but
the public story is the company agent network it enables.

Lead with the pillars: security, automation, and collaboration are the concrete
promise behind the bigger vision.

Use the existing beam language: violet transmission fields and amber live-state
signals are Beamhop's recognizable visual signature.

Sell the big room: the product should feel like a single place where everything
happens, with posts, questions, scheduled work, agent replies, and company memory
moving through one operating plane.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for all text. Respect reduced-motion preferences by
removing continuous movement and preserving readable static composition. Avoid
using color alone for state, especially around security, live status, and calls
to action.
